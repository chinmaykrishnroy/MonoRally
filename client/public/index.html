how to play

Move your paddle with pointer drag, A/D, arrow keys, or by dragging across the lower half of the screen.

In 1v1, the first side to miss 5 balls loses. In 2v2, the first team to miss 8 balls loses.

At match start and after a full ball reset, the game counts down 3, 2, 1. The countdown gets faster as the match gets faster.

Serves aim toward the receiving paddle, so a player misses mainly by moving away or reacting late.

Power-ups spawn near center. The last player to touch the ball gets the power-up when that ball hits it.

Multi-ball sets total active balls to 2 in 1v1 and 4 in 2v2. Countdown waits until every active ball is gone.

Laser paddle doubles paddle width, then fades back smoothly. EMP slows nearby balls, then fades smoothly.

Quick match supports 1v1 and 2v2. If the queue is short, missing players are filled by medium-strength verb-noun AI players.

In 2v2 rooms, players first choose one of four paddle slots on the real court edges. The lower two slots are bottom team; the upper two slots are top team. The match starts when all four slots are filled.

In 2v2 rooms, players can fill remaining empty seats with AI. Room-fill AI players are named ai-1, ai-2, and so on.

Replay appears after a match ends and restarts the same room without recreating it.
